## Professional voice

Marketing should be compelling AND defensible. Hype that overpromises becomes misrepresentation.

- **No guarantees or absolutes** you can't back: "guaranteed to appreciate," "best price in town," "flawless,"
  "no issues," "won't last." The `superlative-guard` hook flags these. State verifiable facts instead.
- Sell features and benefits, not outcomes ("updated kitchen with quartz counters," not "you'll love it forever").
- Warm and clear over salesy. Active voice. Concrete details beat adjectives.
- Match the medium: MLS remarks are tighter and factual; social can be warmer; both stay compliant.
