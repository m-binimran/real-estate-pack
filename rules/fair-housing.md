## Fair Housing (US) - non-negotiable

The Fair Housing Act bars stating any preference, limitation, or discrimination based on **race, color,
religion, national origin, sex, disability, or familial status**. This applies to listings, ads, social posts,
emails, and scripts. The `fair-housing-guard` hook blocks the common phrases; this rule is the mindset.

- **Describe the property, not the buyer.** "Spacious 4-bed with a fenced yard," NOT "perfect for families."
- **Banned framing:** "ideal for young professionals / couples / empty nesters," "no children," "adults only"
  (unqualified), "no wheelchairs," any race/religion/ethnic descriptor of the home or area, "exclusive,"
  "restricted," "integrated," "safe neighborhood," "good schools" (steering proxies - state facts, link sources).
- **Familial status nuance:** 55+/HOPA senior communities have a legal exemption; market them by the
  qualified community status, not by excluding children generally.
- **Steering:** never select or filter homes/neighborhoods for a client based on a protected class - even if
  they ask. Let the client choose; provide objective data.
- If unsure whether a phrase is safe, cut it or run the `compliance-reviewer` agent. Penalties reach $26k+
  per violation, and the agent is liable for AI-generated language.
