# market-report

Turn real market data into a clear, honest story. Every number needs a source.

## Process
1. **Define the market:** geography (zip/neighborhood/city), property type, time window.
2. **Pull the metrics** from a real source (MLS, public records, a named market data provider): active inventory,
   new listings, closed sales, median/avg sale price, $/sqft, median days on market, months of supply,
   sale-to-list ratio. Cite the source and date.
3. **Read the trend:** buyer's vs. seller's market (months of supply), price direction, momentum. Be measured -
   don't overstate.
4. **Make it useful:** what it means for a buyer vs. a seller right now.

## Output
- A short narrative + a small stats table, each figure sourced. Headline takeaway in one line.

## Guardrails
- **No invented or stale numbers** - cite the source and as-of date; mark anything you couldn't verify.
- Avoid predictions stated as certainty ("prices will rise") - the `superlative` rule. Describe trends, note uncertainty.
- Keep neighborhood descriptions factual; no steering proxies ("safe," "good schools" -> link objective data instead).
