# objection-handling

Acknowledge, understand, respond with value and facts - never with pressure or misrepresentation.

## Framework (per objection)
1. **Acknowledge** the concern genuinely.
2. **Clarify** the real issue behind it (ask, don't assume).
3. **Respond with evidence** - data, process, your value - not hype.
4. **Confirm** it's addressed and move forward.

## Common ones
- **"Your commission is too high."** Explain what's included (marketing, negotiation, coordination, net result),
  show value; commissions are negotiable - be straight about that.
- **"We'll wait for the market."** Share objective market data (the `market-report`), not fear.
- **"Zillow says it's worth more."** Walk through the `cma` with real comps; AVMs aren't appraisals.
- **"Why hasn't it sold?"** Price, condition, exposure - honest diagnosis + a plan.
- **FSBO / "we'll sell it ourselves."** Show the gaps you fill; respect their choice.

## Output
- Scripts per objection: acknowledge -> clarify -> evidence-based response -> next step. Keep them honest and brief.

## Guardrails
- No pressure tactics, no false scarcity ("won't last"), no overpromises (the `superlative` rule).
- Commission talk: never imply rates are fixed or set by anyone but the brokerage/agreement.
