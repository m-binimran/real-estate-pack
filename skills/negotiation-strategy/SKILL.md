# negotiation-strategy

Negotiate on interests and evidence, not bluster. Know your client's priorities and their walk-away.

## Process
1. **Clarify your client's priorities:** price, timeline, certainty, terms, contingencies - rank them.
2. **Read leverage:** market (the `market-report` / months of supply), days on market, competing offers,
   the other side's likely motivation (don't assume protected-class anything).
3. **Anchor with support:** open from the `cma`, justify with comps and condition - not a random number.
4. **Trade, don't concede:** give on a low-priority item to win a high-priority one (e.g. price vs. close date
   vs. repairs vs. concessions).
5. **Repairs/appraisal gaps:** quantify, propose credit vs. fix, keep the deal moving.
6. **Define the walk-away** in advance so emotion doesn't drive it.

## Output
- A one-page strategy: client priorities, leverage read, opening + likely counters, trade-offs ranked, and the
  walk-away point. Suggested counter language (factual, not pushy).

## Guardrails
- Strategy, **not legal advice** - the binding effect of terms is the attorney's domain.
- Keep the client's motivation/financials confidential; multiple-offer handling stays fair + Fair-Housing-clean.
- No misrepresentation of other offers or facts to pressure a party.
